# WebQuack Trojan Mouse Construction Diagram

This diagram outlines how to physically wire the Arduino Pro Micro, D1 Mini Pro, and a generic mouse together using a mini USB Hub, creating a "Trojan Mouse." 

When plugged into a target PC, the system will recognize both the generic mouse and the hidden WebQuack (Pro Micro) keyboard.

WIRING NOTES:
=============================================================================================

1. THE USB HUB
   You must use a tiny, inexpensive USB 2.0 hub (e.g., from AliExpress or by cracking open 
   a cheap dollar-store hub). Remove the plastic casing to make it fit inside the mouse.
   Solder the mouse's original long USB cable to the Hub's "Input" pads.

2. PORT 1 (The Real Mouse)
   Solder 4 short wires (5V, GND, D+, D-) from the Hub's Port 1 output pads back to the 
   original mouse circuit board where the long cable used to attach.

3. PORT 2 (WebQuack)
   Solder 4 short wires (5V, GND, D+, D-) from the Hub's Port 2 output pads to the USB 
   pads/pins on the Arduino Pro Micro.
   * Note: The D+ / D- pins are typically on the underside of a standard Pro Micro, 
     or you must carefully solder to the microscopic legs of its micro-USB connector.

4. THE D1 MINI PRO
   Wire the D1 Mini Pro to the Pro Micro exactly as normal WebQuack instructions dictate:
   - Pro Micro VCC (5V) -> D1 Mini 5V
   - Pro Micro GND      -> D1 Mini GND
   - Pro Micro RXI      <- D1 Mini TX
   - Pro Micro TXO      -> D1 Mini RX (Use a 1k Ohm resistor inline to drop 5V to 3.3V)

5. STEALTH
   Wrap the Mini Hub, Pro Micro, and D1 Mini Pro separately in Kapton tape or electrical 
   tape to prevent short circuits before cramming them into the heel of the mouse shell.
